Djäkneberget

The name Djäkneberget comes from djækne, a disciple from the nearby high school (now Rudbeckian high school). Valborg Fair evenings on the mountain with fireworks and fires, but it ceased in the 1830s after an accident. Information and photo source: Wikipedia

Vasaparken/Vasa Park

Originally, the park was a royal garden at the then Munkholmen, which Gustav Vasa had built in 1541. It was opened to the public in the 17th century, when it was used for skate games and other entertainments. Through a royal letter from 1863, the garden was forever opened to the city. Information and photo source: Wikipedia

Västerås rådhus

Västerås rådhus/Västerås City Hall is a building at Fiskartorget in central Västerås. The house was built in 1860. and has a hall on the upper floor with stucco ceilings and large crystal chandeliers. Until 1884, the hall served as the city's official banquet hall. Information and photo source: Wikipedia

Turbinhuset/Turbine House

The great role that the power station had played for Västerås brought a thought to life, in the late 1960s, about turning the Turbinhuset into a museum. One problem that is often encountered in similar projects is the lack of money. But the goal of this project was to re-install a turbine. Information and photo source: Wikipedia

Västerås slott/Västerås Castle

Västerås was formerly called Västra Aros (Västra Åmynningen) and became bishop's seat in the middle of the 12th century. In the 13th century, western Aros became the most important shipping port for the Bergslagen and, together with Stockholm, the main city in the Mälardalen. Information and photo source: Wikipedia
